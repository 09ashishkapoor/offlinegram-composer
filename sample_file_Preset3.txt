1. Shhmashhana kalika: The dark-bodied Goddess of the cremation ground, the power of death and dissolution.
2. Kali: The Black Goddess, ruler of Time.
3. Bhadra kali: The all-auspicious Dark Mother.
4. Kapalini: She who bears the skull, showing Her transcendence over mortality and the cycle of creation.
5. Guhya kali: The hidden and secret Dark Goddess.
6. Maha-kali: The Great Dark Mother, beautiful and compassionate to devotees, yet terrifying to their enemies.
7. Kurukulla-'virodhini: The destroyer of opposition, especially as the one who bestows siddhis and protects those who attain them.
8. Kalika: The tender form of Kali, the Dark One, gentle in presence yet potent in power.
9. Kala ratri cha: She who is the Dark Night of Time, the profound dissolution and ultimate reality beyond all manifestations.
10. Maha-kala nitambini: The dark-limbed one who rests upon Mahakala, the great Lord of Time.
11. Kala bhairava bharya cha: The consort of Kala Bhairava.
12. Kula vartma prakashhini: The illuminator of the path of the spiritual lineage, revealing the essence of the Kaula tradition.
13. Kamada: The fulfiller of all desires.
14. Kamini: The beautiful and desirable Goddess, the object of divine love and devotion.
15. Kamya: She who embodies all longing and aspiration.
16. Kamaniya swa-bhavini: She whose very nature is beauty and loveliness.
17. Kasturi rasa liptanggi: Her body is anointed with the essence of musk.
